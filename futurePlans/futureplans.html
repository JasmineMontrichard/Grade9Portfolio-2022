Future Plans

Planning ahead is a big step towards success. It helps you narrow down what needs to be done and think about the potential outcomes of a decision. It is a good way to come prepared for whatever might happen. Time is most valuable, so we should use it carefully.

Current Plans

High School

My goal is to try and do well in school and participate in events that benefit myself before going into university. It would also be to take courses that can benefit me while going into Computer Science or Artifical Intelligence Engineering. I hope that I can push myself so that I can become successful.

I will also realized I needed to use options such as Night School or Summer School to hopefully all the courses I want to take in the rest of my four years of high school.

Future Career

Currently, I am planning to become an AI Engineer, or a Software Engineer as an alternative. I'm interested in contributing to the development of AI and the development and maintenance of new software.

I am excited to see how far AI will go for as long as I'm alive, but there are some ethical concerns about AI that worry me such as:

  • Technological Singularity — where AI becomes uncontrollable
  • AI's Decision-Making — such as the disregard of life or things to achieve it's goal

It will probably take decades or centuries for any of these to become a serious threat to humanity, but I wouldn't be here by the time it might happen.

Fortunately since 2021, countries around the world are effectively creating a plan to protect humanity from the danger of AI, and that's what is important.

Post-Secondary Education

I am considering the universities and courses that I might want to take after graduating from high school. The dream university that I want to go to is the University of Toronto. If I am not accepted, some alternatives might be McMaster University or University of Waterloo. I would like to be able to take courses for Artificial Intelligence Engineering or Computer Science.

However, for AI Engineering I would have to take a lot of heavy courses such as Grade 12 math and science. It is most likely I would be subjected to take a gap year which I don't find preferable. Hopefully, I will be able to fit my chosen courses into the rest of my four years of high school while taking Summer and Night School.
